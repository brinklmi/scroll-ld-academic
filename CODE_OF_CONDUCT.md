## üõ°Ô∏è Enforcement Responsibilities

### Community Leaders

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that violates this Code of Conduct.

**Enforcement Philosophy**: Like Itachi, we protect through strategic positioning, not force. Violations are corrected through attunement, not punishment.

### Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:
- GitLab repository (issues, merge requests, comments)
- Documentation and wiki
- Community forums and chat
- Public events representing the project
- Private communications when representing the project

## ‚ö° Enforcement Process

### 1. Observation
Community leaders observe potential violations.

### 2. Assessment
Determine if the behavior violates Maat principles:
- Does it distort truth?
- Does it create injustice?
- Does it disrupt balance?
- Does it violate divine order?

### 3. Correction
Apply appropriate correction based on severity:

#### Level 1: Gentle Attunement
**Violation**: Minor misunderstanding or first-time inappropriate behavior  
**Response**: Private message explaining the issue, offering guidance  
**Outcome**: Opportunity to correct with grace

#### Level 2: Formal Warning
**Violation**: Repeated minor violations or single moderate violation  
**Response**: Public or private warning, temporary restrictions  
**Outcome**: Clear expectations set, monitoring period

#### Level 3: Temporary Suspension
**Violation**: Serious violation or pattern of violations  
**Response**: Temporary ban from community spaces (7-30 days)  
**Outcome**: Time for reflection and attunement

#### Level 4: Permanent Removal
**Violation**: Severe violation, sustained harassment, or violation of oath-sealed protocols  
**Response**: Permanent ban from all community spaces  
**Outcome**: Protection of the field takes precedence

### 4. Appeal
Any enforcement decision may be appealed by contacting community leaders. Appeals are reviewed by multiple leaders using Maat principles.

## üìû Reporting Violations

### How to Report

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **For Technical Issues**: Open an issue with `code-of-conduct` label
2. **For Sensitive Matters**: Email community leaders directly (contact info in README)
3. **For Urgent Concerns**: Contact project maintainers immediately

### What to Include

- Description of the violation
- When and where it occurred
- Any relevant screenshots or evidence
- Impact on you or the community
- Desired outcome (if any)

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion. Community leaders will protect the identity of reporters unless disclosure is required for resolution.
